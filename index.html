The City of Palaces

Mysore, officially known as Mysuru, is the second-most populous city in Karnataka, India, renowned for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance. Often referred to as the "City of Palaces," it boasts numerous ornate structures, with the Mysore Palace being the most iconic. The city is also famous for its annual Dasara festival, a ten-day event featuring grand processions, music, and dance, attracting visitors from around the world.

Must visit places in and around Mysore

Mysuru Palace

A stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, this palace is the city's most iconic landmark.

Chamundi Hills

Home to the Chamundeshwari Temple, the hill provides panoramic views of Mysore and is a significant religious site.

Mysuru Zoo

Established in 1892, Mysore Zoo, officially known as Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is one of India's oldest and most renowned zoos.

Brindavan Gardens

Located 16 km from the city center, these gardens are renowned for their illuminated fountains and musical fountain show in the evenings.

Devaraja Market

A vibrant market offering a glimpse into local life, with stalls selling fresh produce, spices, and traditional items.

Karanji Lake

A serene spot ideal for nature lovers, featuring a butterfly park and an aviary, making it a perfect place for bird watching and leisurely walks.

Jaganmohan Palace

An art gallery housing a vast collection of paintings and artifacts, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Somnathpur Temple

Located about 35 kilometers from Mysore, this 13th-century temple is a fine example of Hoysala architecture, known for its intricate carvings and detailed sculptures.

These destinations provide a mix of cultural, historical, and natural experiences, enriching your visit to the Mysore region.
